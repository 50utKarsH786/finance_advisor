Company: Microsoft
Period: Fiscal Year 2026 Q1
Source Type: Investor Relations performance summary

Overview:
Microsoft reported strong first-quarter fiscal 2026 performance with growth across all segments.

Key Metrics:
Revenue Growth: Up 18% year over year
Operating Income Growth: Up 24% year over year
Revenue Increase: $12.1 billion
Operating Income Increase: $7.4 billion

Segment Notes:
Intelligent Cloud revenue increased, driven by Azure.
Productivity and Business Processes revenue increased, driven by Microsoft 365 Commercial cloud.
More Personal Computing revenue increased, helped by Windows OEM and Search and news advertising, partly offset by weaker hardware businesses.

Cost and Margin Notes:
Cost of revenue increased 20%, driven by Microsoft Cloud growth.
Microsoft Cloud gross margin percentage decreased to 68%.
The company said the margin change was affected by scaling AI infrastructure and growing AI feature usage.
Operating expenses increased 5%, driven by cloud and AI engineering investments.

Management Interpretation:
Microsoft showed broad-based growth across segments.
Cloud and AI investment remained a major theme in the quarter.
The company continued increasing spending on AI infrastructure and talent.
