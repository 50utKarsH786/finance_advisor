Risk Analysis Framework

1. Business Risk:
Check whether the company depends heavily on one product, one service, or one customer segment.

2. Competition Risk:
Evaluate whether strong competitors can reduce pricing power, growth, or market share.

3. Regulatory Risk:
Consider whether laws, compliance requirements, or government action can affect operations.

4. Supply Chain Risk:
Review dependence on suppliers, manufacturing concentration, logistics, and raw material availability.

5. Margin Pressure:
Check whether costs are rising faster than revenue, which can reduce profitability.

6. Debt Risk:
Assess how much debt the company carries and whether repayment burden may hurt stability.

7. Currency Risk:
For global companies, exchange-rate movements can affect revenue and profits.

8. Execution Risk:
Management may fail to deliver strategy, product launches, expansion plans, or efficiency targets.

9. Macroeconomic Risk:
High inflation, recession, interest rate changes, or weak consumer demand can affect performance.

10. Concentration Risk:
The company may rely too much on one geography, vendor, or customer type.
