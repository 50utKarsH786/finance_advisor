Finance Glossary

Revenue:
The total money a company earns from its business activities before expenses are deducted.

Net Income:
The profit left after all expenses, taxes, and interest are subtracted from revenue.

EPS (Earnings Per Share):
A company's profit divided by the number of outstanding shares. EPS is a key profitability metric.

Operating Income:
Profit earned from core business operations before interest and taxes.

Gross Margin:
The percentage of revenue left after subtracting the cost of goods sold. It shows basic production profitability.

Free Cash Flow:
Cash generated by a company after accounting for capital expenditures. It indicates financial flexibility.

Market Capitalization:
The total market value of a company's outstanding shares. It is often calculated as share price multiplied by shares outstanding.

Dividend:
A payment made by a company to shareholders, usually from profits.

Debt:
Money borrowed by a company that must be repaid, often with interest.

Equity:
The residual ownership value in a company after liabilities are subtracted from assets.
