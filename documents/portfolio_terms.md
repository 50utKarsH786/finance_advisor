Portfolio Terms

Diversification:
Spreading investments across different assets to reduce overall risk.

Asset Allocation:
How investments are divided among categories such as stocks, bonds, and cash.

Volatility:
The degree to which prices move up and down over time.

Beta:
A measure of how much a stock tends to move relative to the broader market.

Drawdown:
The decline from a portfolio's peak value to a later low point.

CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate):
The annualized growth rate of an investment over multiple years.

Risk-Return Tradeoff:
Higher expected returns usually come with higher risk.

Rebalancing:
Adjusting portfolio weights back to target allocation after market movement.

Liquidity:
How easily an asset can be bought or sold without greatly affecting its price.

Time Horizon:
The length of time an investor expects to hold an investment before needing the money.
